Design Philosophy

Design is never decoration.
It's how clarity
becomes action.

My approach to design is grounded in engineering discipline and human empathy - two forces that together produce work that is both beautiful and functional.

01

Problem-First Thinking

I resist jumping to solutions. Every project begins with a deep investigation of the problem space - stakeholder interviews, competitive analysis, and usage data tell the real story before a single frame is drawn.

02

Human-Centered, Engineer-Informed

My engineering background means I design with implementation in mind. Beautiful interactions that can't be built serve no one. I push creative boundaries within the bounds of what's genuinely possible.

03

Systems Over Surfaces

I think in systems, not screens. A cohesive design language scales better, ships faster, and maintains integrity across teams and platforms over time.

About Me

Designer.
Engineer.
Builder.

"I became a designer because engineering taught me to love problems. I build products because design taught me how to solve them beautifully."

I started in engineering - learning to think in systems, constraints, and logic. But it was design that taught me the part engineering couldn't: that the best solutions are the ones people actually want to use.

Over the past five years, I've designed everything from zero-to-one mobile apps and browser extensions to full brand identities and design systems. I've worked across product, marketing, and developer contexts - giving me a rare multi-dimensional view of what makes digital products work.

In addition, I participate in critique circles, share process breakdowns, and mentor up-and-coming designers who are juggling their craft and careers.
